Decision Function: $f(X)$

Loss Function: $L(Y, f(X))$

Risk Function: $R(Y, f(X))=E[L(Y, f(X))]$

We want to find a Decision Function which minimizes our Risk Function.

Squaerd Error Loss: $L(Y, f(X))=(Y-f(X))^2$

$X \in \mathbb{R}^n$, $Y \in \mathbf{R}$

$L(Y, f(X))=(Y-f(X))^2$

$\begin{aligned}
\text{EPE}(f)&=\text{E}[(Y-f(X))^2] \\\\
&=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}(y-f(x))^2f_{X, Y}(x, y)dxdy \\\\
&=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}(y-f(x))^2f_{Y|X}(y|x)f_X(x)dxdy \\\\
&=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\left\[\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}(y-f(x))^2f_{Y|X}(y|x)dy\right\]f_X(x)dx \\\\
&=\text{E}_X\left\[\text{E}_{Y|X}[(Y-f(X))^2]|X\right\]
\end{aligned}$

$\begin{aligned}
f(x)&=\text{argmin}_c\text{E}\_{Y|X}(\[Y-c\]^2|X=x) \\\\
&=\text{E}(Y|X=x)
\end{aligned}$

$\hat{Y}(x)=\dfrac{1}{k}\displaystyle\sum_{x_i \in N_k(x)}y_i$

At each point $x$, we might ask for the average of all those $y_i$s with input $x_i=x$.

Since there is typically at most one observation at any point $x$, we settle for $\hat{f}(x)=\text{Ave}(y_i|x_i \in N_k(x))$
